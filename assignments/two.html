Software Development & Architecture

Software development and architecture involve structuring systems for scalability, maintainability, and performance through key phases like requirement analysis, design, implementation, and testing. Key content areas include architectural styles (microservices, layered), design principles (SOLID, DRY), and managing non-functional requirements (security, performance).

Software Architecture

  • Structural Components:

    • Organizing systems into layers, modules, and components with defined interfaces

  • Non-Functional Requirements (Quality Attributes):

    • Scalability, maintainability, reliability, security, and performance.

  • Documentation & Visualization:

    • Using UML diagrams, C4 models, and ADRs (Architectural Decision Records) to document, communicate, and justify decisions.
